Persona Command Center — Core Definition (Version 2)

Purpose

Persona Command Center exists for one reason:

Grow four high-quality X accounts with the least possible human effort and the lowest sustainable operating cost.

Everything in the system should support that objective.

Nothing more.

Nothing less.

⸻

The Core Promise

Persona Command Center continuously transforms today’s conversations into publish-ready opportunities.

Hermes discovers and researches opportunities.

Persona Command Center helps the operator choose, refine, publish, and measure them.

The operator should never need to hunt for ideas.

The system should deliver them.

⸻

Success Metric

Success is not measured by:

* articles collected
* providers integrated
* dashboards created
* research completed

Success is measured by:

* high-quality posts published
* account growth
* engagement
* impressions
* followers gained
* time saved

⸻

Production Goals

Week 1

Produce 4 high-quality posts per day across four personas.

Each post should be based on a real opportunity discovered that day.

The operator should spend no more than a few minutes selecting and refining each post.

⸻

Week 2

Increase throughput while maintaining quality.

Target:

8 high-quality posts per day.

⸻

Week 3

Operate at production scale.

Target:

12 high-quality posts per day

without materially increasing operator effort.

The increase should come from better automation, not more manual work.

⸻

Core Workflow

Hermes performs continuous monitoring.

For each persona:

1. Monitor Watch List.
2. Determine whether a watched entity posted today.
3. Determine whether a watched entity was mentioned today.
4. Detect relevant topic opportunities.
5. Research and verify.
6. Produce one Intelligence Packet.

Persona Command Center:

1. Receives Intelligence Packets.
2. Ranks opportunities.
3. Presents the best opportunities to the Operator.
4. Generates multiple posting angles.
5. Allows rapid editing and approval.
6. Tracks publication and performance.

⸻

Intelligence Priority

Every Intelligence Packet should prioritize information in this order.

Priority 1

The watched entity posted today.

This is the strongest signal.

Priority 2

The watched entity was mentioned today.

Examples:

* news
* X discussions
* RSS
* Crawl4AI
* SearchAgent discoveries

Priority 3

A topic relevant to the persona accelerated today.

Examples:

AI

Datacenters

Semiconductors

Energy

Priority 4

Background information.

Background should never dominate today’s opportunity.

⸻

Personas

A Persona teaches Hermes:

Who am I?

What topics matter to me?

Who should I watch?

Everything else is implementation detail.

⸻

Hermes

Hermes is the Intelligence Operating System.

Hermes:

* monitors
* searches
* researches
* verifies
* scores
* generates Intelligence Packets

Hermes owns intelligence generation.

⸻

Persona Command Center

Persona Command Center is the Operator Operating System.

It:

* stores configuration
* receives Intelligence Packets
* presents opportunities
* generates drafts
* manages approvals
* schedules posts
* records performance

It never performs large-scale intelligence gathering.

⸻

Design Principle

Every engineering decision must answer one question:

Does this reduce the effort required to publish another excellent X post today?

If the answer is no, it does not belong in Persona Command Center.

⸻

Simplicity Principle

Simple is always better.

The operator should never configure implementation details.

The operator teaches Hermes:

* what matters
* who matters

Hermes determines:

* where to search
* how to verify
* how to build Intelligence Packets

⸻

Definition of Done

Persona Command Center succeeds when an operator can reliably publish:

* 4 high-quality posts/day during Week 1,
* 8 high-quality posts/day during Week 2,
* 12 high-quality posts/day during Week 3,

while spending dramatically less time finding opportunities than writing about them.

The system should feel less like a dashboard and more like having a professional research team preparing today’s posting opportunities before the operator sits down.
