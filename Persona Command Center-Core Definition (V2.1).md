Persona Command Center — Core Definition (Version 2.1)

Purpose

Persona Command Center exists for one reason:

Grow four high-quality X accounts by continuously identifying today’s best posting opportunities before competitors do, with the least human effort and the lowest sustainable operating cost.

Everything in the system should support that objective.

Nothing more.

Nothing less.

⸻

The Core Promise

Every day Hermes should answer one question:

“What should each persona post about today?”

Persona Command Center should then make it possible to review, refine, publish, and measure those opportunities in minutes.

The operator should never need to hunt for ideas.

The system should deliver them.

If there is no worthwhile opportunity, the system should remain silent.

⸻

Success Metric

Success is not measured by:

* articles collected
* providers integrated
* research completed
* dashboards created
* signals generated

Success is measured by:

* high-quality posts published
* account growth
* impressions
* engagement
* followers gained
* operator time saved
* cost per published post

⸻

Production Goals

Week 1

Publish:

4 high-quality posts per day

One per persona.

Every post should originate from a real opportunity detected that day.

Operator time:

Less than five minutes per post.

⸻

Week 2

Increase throughput while maintaining quality.

Target:

8 high-quality posts per day

Automation should increase.

Operator effort should not.

⸻

Week 3

Operate at production scale.

Target:

12 high-quality posts per day

The increase must come from better opportunity detection and automation—not additional manual work.

⸻

Product Philosophy

Persona Command Center is not a research database.

It is not a news reader.

It is not a dashboard.

It is an Opportunity Engine.

The system exists to continuously detect today’s highest-value posting opportunities.

Everything else is supporting infrastructure.

⸻

Core Workflow

Hermes performs continuous monitoring.

For every Watch List item:

1. Did the entity post today?
2. If not, was the entity mentioned today?
3. If not, is a monitored topic accelerating today?
4. If none of the above are true, produce nothing.

If an opportunity exists:

Opportunity Priority

Every Opportunity Packet should prioritize information in this order.

Priority 1

The watched entity posted today.

This is the strongest possible signal.

Examples:

Andrej Karpathy posted.

Sam Altman posted.

Jensen Huang posted.

⸻

Priority 2

The watched entity is being discussed today.

Examples:

News

RSS

X conversations

SearchAgent

Crawl4AI

Only same-day discussion is relevant.

⸻

Priority 3

A monitored topic accelerated today.

Examples:

AI

Datacenters

Semiconductors

Energy

Cybersecurity

The topic must represent a genuine opportunity—not background information.

⸻

Priority 4

Background context.

Background exists only to improve today’s opportunity.

It should never dominate the packet.

⸻

Personas

A Persona teaches Hermes three things:

Who am I?

What topics matter to me?

Who should I watch?

Nothing else.

The Persona page should remain intentionally simple.

⸻

Hermes

Hermes is the Intelligence Operating System.

Hermes:

* monitors
* detects
* researches
* verifies
* scores
* produces Opportunity Packets

Hermes owns intelligence generation.

Hermes determines:

* where to search
* which providers to use
* how to verify information
* how much supporting evidence is required

⸻

Persona Command Center

Persona Command Center is the Operator Operating System.

It:

* stores persona configuration
* receives Opportunity Packets
* ranks opportunities
* generates posting angles
* supports editing
* manages approval
* records publishing
* tracks performance

Persona Command Center does not perform large-scale intelligence gathering.

Hermes does.

⸻

Simplicity Principle

Simple is always better.

Every additional field, button, workflow, or configuration option must justify its existence.

The operator should teach Hermes:

* what matters
* who matters

Hermes should determine everything else.

⸻

Design Principle

Every engineering decision must answer one question:

Does this help the operator publish another excellent X post today with less effort, lower cost, or higher quality?

If the answer is no, it does not belong in Persona Command Center.

⸻

Learning Loop

Every published post becomes feedback.

Track:

Opportunity Packet

↓

Published

↓

Performance

↓

Lessons

↓

Future ranking improvements

The system should become better at identifying winning opportunities over time.

⸻

Definition of Done

Persona Command Center succeeds when Hermes continuously delivers high-quality posting opportunities and the operator can reliably publish:

* 4 high-quality posts/day during Week 1
* 8 high-quality posts/day during Week 2
* 12 high-quality posts/day during Week 3

while spending dramatically less time finding opportunities than writing about them.

The product should feel less like operating software and more like having a professional research team that arrives every morning with today’s best posting opportunities already prepared.
